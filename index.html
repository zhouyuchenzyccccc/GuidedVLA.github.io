Specifying Task-Relevant Factors via Plug-and-Play Action Attention Specialization

Abstract

Vision-Language-Action (VLA) models often rely on implicit end-to-end supervision, which leads to spurious correlations and poor generalization. We propose GuidedVLA, a framework that explicitly guides the action decoder to focus on task-relevant factors via attention head specialization. By supervising object grounding, temporal skill logic, and geometric perception on dedicated heads, GuidedVLA significantly improves robustness and generalization across simulation and real-robot benchmarks.

Method

GuidedVLA treats the action decoder as an assembly of functional components. Specific attention heads are supervised to specialize in: object grounding, skill recognition, and geometry perception. A ControlNet-style residual adapter enables plug-and-play guidance without disrupting pretrained backbones.

Experiments

  • LIBERO-Plus robustness benchmark
  • RobotWin 2.0 multi-task evaluation
  • Real-world experiments on ALOHA AgileX and PSI-Bot
